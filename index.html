Car accidents happened in Victoria between 2015-2019

Visualisation created by Felix Chow, Last updated 2023

During year 2015-2019, Vicotria has more than 60000 (60690) car accidents and around 80000 (79394) death or injuries. Below is the map that shows the location of differnt car accidents occured during 2015-2019, feel free to zoom in zoom out to have a idea of the distribution of car accidents happened in Victoria during those year.

Driving under the influence (DUI), or impaired driving, refers to drinking alcohol and then operating a motor vehicle. Motor vehicles are not limited to just cars; this also includes bicycles, motorcycles, golf carts, boats, jet-skis, and lawn mowers.


Luckily in Victoria, the amount of DUI is not high, around 3% of the car accidents in Victoria during 2015-2019 is due to DUI. Hopefully the number will stay low or even lower.

We analysed the data and found that Melbourn city, Geelong and Casey is the top 3 places in Victoria with high amount of car accidents happened during 2015-2019.

Interestingly, even Melbourn city has the highest amount of car accidents compare to other location in Victoria, the amount of injuries or death is not the highest, whereas Geelong have >20% less accidents happened compared with Melbourn city, the number of injuryies or death is 2 times higher than it.

Also, Yarra Ranges has a relatively low number of accidents but a high amount of injury or death rate.

When we look at which speed zones do most of the accidents happened in. Not suprised that 100 km/hr and 110 km/hr zone is the top 2 speed zone where most traffic accidents happened in.

However, what interesting is the 3rd speed zone where most accident happened in is the 20 km/hr speed zone.

Low-speed accidents may not seem as severe as high-speed collisions, but they still carry the risk of causing serious injuries. Even if a vehicle is traveling at a slow speed, the impact can create enough force to cause significant harm to the occupants inside the car. Some common injuries that can occur in low-speed accidents include whiplash, concussions, and broken bones.
